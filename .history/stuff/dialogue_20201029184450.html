Dialogue

General conversation

If you are starting learning Polish language you might face many difficult phrases and grammar expressions. Before we will start more advanced things, let’s try to remember most common used phrases in daily life. After each dialogues you can complete simple tasks, which will be on the bottom of the conversations.

Saying Hi and Bye!

Good morning/ Good afternoon. – Dzień dobry.
Good evening. – Dobry wieczór.
Hi/ Hello! - Hej/ Cześć!
Good bye. – Do widzenia.
Bye! – Pa/ Cześć!
See you later. – Do (zobaczenia) potem.
See you tomorrow. – Do jutra.
It was nice to see you. – Miło było cię zobaczyć/ widzieć.


First meeting

Nice to meet you. – Miło Cię poznać.
My name is... - Mam na imię…/ Nazywam się...
Please. - Proszę.
You’re welcome. – Proszę.
You’re very welcome. – Proszę bardzo.
I’m a student. – Jestem studentem.


Basic social phrases

How are you? - Jak się masz?
How are you doing? - Jak się czujesz?
What’s up? - Jak leci?
I’m fine. – W porządku/ Dobrze.
I’m not too bad. – Nieźle.
I’m not really good/ I’m bad. – Źle/ Fatalnie.
And how are you? - A co u Ciebie?
Thank you. - Dziękuję.
Thank you very much. – Dziękuję bardzo.
Thanks. – Dzięki.
I’m sorry. - Przepraszam.
I’m really sorry. – Bardzo przepraszam/ Przepraszam bardzo.
Excuse me. – Przepraszam.
Cheers! - Na zdrowie


Extra

Yes - Tak
No – Nie
Hello? (over the phone) – Halo?
I don’t understand. – Nie rozumiem.
I understand. - Rozumiem.
What does it mean? – Co to znaczy?
What does … mean? - Co znaczy …?
Can you repeat? - Czy możesz powtórzyć?
Sorry, can you speak slower? – Przepraszam, czy możesz mówić wolniej?
I can’t speak polish. – Nie mówię po polsku.
I can speak polish. – Mówię po polsku.
I don’t speak polish much. – Nie mówię dobrze po polsku.


Questions

Who? – Kto?
What? - Co? Why? - Dlaczego/ Czemu?
When? - Kiedy?
Where? - Gdzie?
How? - Jak?
What time? - O której?
How much? - Ile?

Polish Conversation – First meeting

In the previous dialogue you have learnt simple phrases for daily conversations. You can show your gratitude or apologize. When someone is talking too fast you can ask that person, to talk slowly. Now, when you know some more words – it’s time for more vocabulary! Polish is not easy, but learning new skills is always fun. When you will be facing some difficulties just try to imagine your first successful conversation… How does it feel? When you are finally able to communicate with someone and you can understand most of the phrases (or even everything)! Let’s try to learn something new today!
As you can see in many books about Polish gramma… We have not only verb conjugation, but same verb can be different – depending is male (M) or female (F) is currently speaking. Seems difficult? It’s not that hard. Everything is explained in gramma section, so we can skip explanation and just focus on learning new phrases. ARE YOU READY?

Grocery shopping

In this lesson we are taking you on a trip to a local grocery store. In a lot of smaller stores there’s no self-service and you have to convey what do you want to buy to the shopkeeper. There’s a dialogue, some vocabulary and a short exercise you should have no problem with after this lesson. Also you should recognize some vocabulary from the previous lessons.



Klient: Dzień dobry!
Sprzedawca: Dzień dobry! Co podać?
Klient: Proszę makaron penne i tuńczyka.
Sprzedawca: Ile?
Klient: Paczkę makaronu i dwie puszki tuńczyka.
Sprzedawca: Proszę. I co jeszcze?
Klient: Proszę jeszcze cebulę i śmietanę kremówkę.
Sprzedawca: Proszę. To wszystko?
Klient: Tak, dziękuję. Ile płacę?
Sprzedawca: Czternaście siedemdziesiąt. Kartą czy gotówką?
Klient: Zapłacę kartą.
Sprzedawca: Czy drukować potwierdzenie?
Klient: Nie, dziękuję. Do widzenia.
Sprzedawca: Dziękuję. Do widzenia.

Customer: Good morning!
Shopkeeper: Good morning! How can I help you?
Customer: I would like penne pasta and some tuna.
Shopkeeper: How many?
Customer: A pack of penne pasta and two cans of tuna.
Shopkeeper: Here you are. Anything else?
Customer: I would like an onion and heavy cream.
Shopkeeper: Here you are. Is that all?
Customer: Yes, thank you. How much is it?
Shopkeeper: Fourteen seventy. Would you like to pay with a card or cash?
Customer: I’ll pay the card.
Shopkeeper: Do you want a payment confirmation?
Customer: No, thank you. Good bye!
Shopkeeper: Goodbye!

So from this dialogue you already have learned some new things:

forma płatności (fɔrma pwatnɔɕt͡ɕi) - payment method : gotówka - cash

karta - card
