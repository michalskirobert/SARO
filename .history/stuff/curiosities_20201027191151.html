Pizza with ketchup?

08.01.2020

The most popular pizza type is the one on very thin crust, served with a bit of good olive oil In Poland, you also eat such a pizza, but usually in restaurants.
So what kind of pizza do Polish people are eating at home? Where did our belief that pizzas are eaten with ketchup come from? Who perpetrated such barbarism? Who would think of putting a ton of tomato sauce on a delicious, thin pizza?
Thin crust? Well‚Ä¶ Not in Poland! In many Polish homes,pizza is made on thick dough, resembling thick, fluffy and aromatic foccacia. Making such a dough takes some time, but it's worth it.

The most standard recipe is:

* Put a whole cube of fresh yeast into 100ml of warm water and a little sugar, so that the yeast can rise well,
* Then you can add a little milk to whiten, olive oil and salt
* Finally, pour in the flour - half a kilo is the minimum until a sticky mass is formed. To make the dough more fluffy, let the mass rise for a few hours.
...

On the baking tray, when you will put the dough, you can put a layer of tomato sauce and your favorite ingredients. Due to the fact that the dough is thick - you can put a looooot of them. The baked pizza is thick and fluffy, but the dough itself is a bit dry. 1/3 of the pizza are ingredients, but all the rest is pure yeast dough. Perhaps this is the reason why in Poland such a pizza is served with various sauces. Pizza with garlic sauce and ketchup is very popular.
